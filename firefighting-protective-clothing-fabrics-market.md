<p><ul><li>Inherent Fabric</li><li>Treated Fabric</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Firefighting protective clothing fabrics are categorized into two main types: inherent fabrics and treated fabrics. Inherent fabrics are made from fibers that possess fire-resistant properties inherently, offering long-lasting protection without needing additional treatments. Examples include aramid and PBI fibers. Treated fabrics, on the other hand, are typically made from conventional materials that undergo chemical processes to enhance their fire resistance. While treated fabrics may provide versatility and cost-effectiveness, inherent fabrics are often preferred for their durability and reliable performance in extreme conditions.</p></p>
