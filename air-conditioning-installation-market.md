<p><p>The air conditioning installation market features two primary types: whole-home systems and split systems. Whole-home systems, often central air conditioning units, provide uniform cooling throughout an entire residence via ductwork. In contrast, split systems consist of an outdoor condenser and indoor evaporator units, allowing for targeted cooling in specific areas without extensive ducting. Each type caters to different needs and preferences, with whole-home systems suited for larger spaces and split systems offering flexibility and efficiency for individual rooms.</p></p>
