<p><ul><li>Subcutaneous Immunotherapy</li><li>Sublingual Immunotherapy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Asthma immunotherapies primarily include subcutaneous and sublingual immunotherapy. Subcutaneous immunotherapy involves the administration of allergens via injections, gradually increasing the dose to build the body's tolerance, providing long-term relief from asthma symptoms. In contrast, sublingual immunotherapy involves placing allergens under the tongue in tablet or drop form, promoting a similar desensitization effect without the need for injections. Both methods aim to modify the immune response, reducing asthma severity and reliance on medications, benefiting patients through personalized treatment approaches.</p></p>
