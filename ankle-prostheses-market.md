<p><ul><li>Cementless</li><li>Cemented</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The ankle prostheses market is divided into two main types: cemented and cementless prostheses. Cemented prostheses utilize a bone cement to securely attach the implant to the bone, offering immediate stability and strength. Conversely, cementless prostheses rely on a porous surface that allows for bone ingrowth, promoting long-term stability without the use of cement. Each type has its distinct advantages, catering to different patient needs and anatomical considerations, ultimately influencing the choice of implantation method in ankle replacement surgeries.</p></p>
