<p><p>Sushi restaurants can be categorized mainly into traditional and conveyor belt types. Traditional sushi restaurants emphasize authentic dining experiences, where skilled chefs prepare sushi à la carte and often interact with customers, focusing on quality and presentation. In contrast, conveyor belt sushi restaurants, also known as kaiten-zushi, feature an automated system where sushi is displayed on a rotating conveyor, allowing customers to choose dishes at their own pace. This model typically offers a more casual and affordable dining experience.</p></p>
